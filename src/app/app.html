Understanding Peripheral Nerve Surgery of the Hand

What Is Peripheral Nerve Surgery of the Hand?

Peripheral nerve surgery of the hand is performed to treat injuries or compression of nerves in the hand, wrist or forearm that cause pain, numbness, weakness or loss of function. These conditions may affect sensation, muscle control or coordination of the hand.
The goal of peripheral nerve surgery of the hand is to relieve pressure on affected nerves, repair damaged nerve tissue and restore hand function as safely as possible. Depending on the diagnosis, surgery may involve nerve release, repair, or reconstruction using microsurgical techniques.
Not all peripheral nerve-related hand problems require surgery. Careful clinical evaluation is always the first step.

Symptoms When to Visit Sapiens Clinic

You should consult a hand nerve specialist if you experience:

  • Persistent numbness or tingling in fingers or hand
  • Weak grip strength or difficulty holding objects
  • Hand or wrist pain radiating to the arm
  • Loss of sensation or coordination
  • Muscle wasting or visible weakness in the hand
  • Symptoms worsening at night or during repetitive work

A timely consultation with Dr. Darshan Kumar A. Jain, an experienced orthopedic hand surgeon in Malleshwaram, Bangalore, Karnataka, helps identify peripheral nerve involvement early and prevent long-term damage.

Who Is This Surgery For?

Peripheral nerve surgery of the hand may be recommended for patients who:

  • Have nerve compression not improving with medication or splinting
  • Experience progressive numbness or hand weakness
  • Have nerve injuries due to trauma or cuts
  • Show nerve damage on nerve conduction studies or imaging
Require precise hand function for work or daily activities
